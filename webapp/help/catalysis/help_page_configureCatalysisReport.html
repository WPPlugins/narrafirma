Configure catalysis report

Use this page to exclude some questions and sample sizes from your catalysis report.

What to do here

Choose a lower threshold for story subsets. Twenty is a good default for most projects.

Choose which questions you want to include in your catalysis report. Any questions left unchecked here will not appear in the Explore patterns page.

Frequently-asked questions
Why would I want to exclude questions from my catalysis report?

Sometimes questions don't turn out to be very useful, because people didn't understand them, or the answer possibilities you gave didn't appeal to people, or there wasn't as much variety as you expected to find. For whatever reason, you may not want to include every question from your story form in your catalysis report.

Why would I want to exclude stories from statistical tests?

If you have lots of stories, you might want to strengthen the results of your statistical tests by removing all small story subsets from comparison. The higher this threshold the stronger, and fewer, your statistical results will be. Be aware that choosing a threshold below twenty will result in some test results with weaker power.

Can I change my mind about the question exclusions later?

Any question you uncheck here will not appear on the Explore patterns page. But if you include a question, write some observations about it, exclude it, and then change your mind and include it again, the observations you wrote will still be stored in the report. Excluding a question doesn't delete any observations you made about it; it just makes them invisible.

What if I change my mind about the statistical threshold later?

Changing your statistical threshold might change some test values. If you do change this value, be careful to update any observations you've already written that depend on statistical test values.
